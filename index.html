Important information & FAQ

Practical points that remain relevant throughout the meeting

The event is not open to the general public, but only to members of selected 99.9th-percentile-plus (3.090 σ ↥) HIQ societies and their guests. See the Registration page for more information.
Logistical details such as accommodation, travel, venue access and the programme are documented elsewhere and can be accessed through the site navigation.

🚭 Smoking

Smoking and vaping are not permitted in the building or the courtyard.
Please be considerate towards participants who are sensitive to smoke. When smoking outside, keep an appropriate distance from entrances, windows and other people, and avoid exposing others to smoke unnecessarily.

📸 Photography and Privacy

During the meeting, participants may take photographs and videos for private personal use. However, publishing, sharing or otherwise making available any image or video in which another participant is identifiable requires that person's explicit prior consent. Silence, lack of objection or the absence of a red lanyard do not constitute consent.
A red lanyard indicates that the wearer does not wish to be photographed, even for private use. Please respect this wish.
Participants are responsible for ensuring their own compliance with applicable privacy and data protection laws, including the GDPR. The organisers cannot monitor or control the actions of individual participants and accept no responsibility for privacy or GDPR-related issues arising from photographs or videos taken, shared or published by attendees.

💐 Respect the People Doing the Work

egg is a meeting created by members for members, and it only happens because volunteers invest a considerable amount of their personal time and effort.
Nobody is paid to organise, support or run the event.
Please treat volunteers with the same courtesy and respect you would expect yourself. Be patient when help is needed, understand that resources are limited, and remember that every contribution is made voluntarily. By participating, welcoming newcomers and supporting one another, you help make egg a better experience for everyone.
➡️ Respect also means being mindful of volunteer workload, including consulting this wiki before asking questions.

To ensure smooth coordination and technical stability, only official team members (identifiable by the red event logo) are authorised to communicate with venue staff regarding organisational matters.
Participants are kindly asked not to contact venue personnel directly or operate any technical equipment, including mixing consoles, lighting controls or accessing technical areas. If you have a request or notice a need, please contact a team member.

🫶🏻 Respect Vulnerabilities of Other Participants

egg is often described as having a uniquely familiar atmosphere. People speak openly about personal matters and vulnerabilities, and there is frequently generosity towards less well-off participants.
On rare occasions, some participants have felt that this openness was exploited, for example when private conversations later turned into unsolicited professional approaches, or when social generosity was perceived as being deliberately taken advantage of. There is a large grey area here; please err on the side of caution.

👌 Respect Others

We ask everyone not to reinforce the stereotype of highly gifted people as socially insensitive or awkward, especially when identifiable as part of the egg community. Courtesy, patience and basic social consideration are expected throughout the meeting.

🙋🏻‍♂️ Unannounced Guests

No unannounced or unregistered guests are allowed, regardless of how briefly someone intends to attend. If a person is present at the meeting, that person must be properly registered.

🐉 Animals

Participants are strongly advised to leave pets at home. No animals of any kind are permitted on the premises except for service animals that have been formally approved in advance by the organisers. All other animals—including emotional support animals, therapy animals and pets—will not be allowed entry.

🚂 Getting There

Please consider the environment when travelling and prefer trains over aeroplanes whenever possible. Alternatively, share a car. Frankfurt Airport is connected to local and international rail networks and offers a direct S-Bahn connection (S8 and S9) to Konstablerwache in less than 20 minutes for under €7.

🎹 Open Stage

We are planning to host our usual Open Stage, with more information to follow soon. If you have musical skills, would like to showcase something cultural (such as poetry readings or folk dance), or possess a unique talent that you would like to share, there are still open slots available.
Contact us at: open_stage@3090.eu

💬 Is There an Online Group for Participants?

We have a Discourse forum at Discourse where all registered participants are invited to join.

🕑 At What Time Does the Meeting Start and End?

As egg Thursday is a public holiday in some countries, the meeting begins on Thursday. Doors open at 10:00, and as people arrive the meeting gradually gets underway. By evening, a good number of participants are usually present. There is no formal programme on Thursday; some people go out for dinner, while others remain at the venue to welcome new arrivals. The venue closes at 22:00.
> On Sunday, participants generally gather again around 10:00. Those who went out on Saturday night may arrive later, while others may leave early to catch flights. Again, there is no organised programme—just conversation and socialising. The meeting officially ends at 14:00 on Sunday, although some participants may remain afterwards.

In short: Thursday 10:00 to Sunday 14:00.

Germany Tips

Recycling

Recycle bottles and cans - do not throw them in the garbage! Try to recycle as much as possible, and use the appropriate bins for paper, plastic, glass, and other materials.

  • Deposit bottles
  • Recycling in Germany

Restaurants & Tipping

In Germany, restaurant bills are usually paid individually, with each person paying for their own food and drinks.
For good service, it is customary to leave a small tip of around 5–10%.

Tax Free Shopping

Enjoy tax-free shopping in Germany. Bring your passport and purchase items at participating stores to get a refund on the VAT you paid.

  • Tax Free Shopping

Do you need a visa?

Citizens of visa-waiver countries can stay in Germany for up to 90 days without a visa.
All others require a Schengen visa.
